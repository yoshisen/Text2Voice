FAQ

Which languages and voices are supported?

This tool is based on Microsoft Edge TTS and supports dozens of languages such as Chinese (Mandarin and Cantonese), English, Japanese, Korean, French, German, and Spanish. Each language provides multiple voice options, including male and female voices.

What format is the converted audio?

The converted audio is in MP3 format. It can be played online directly or downloaded to your local device and is compatible with all major players and devices.

Is there a text length limit?

The local app no longer enforces a strict character limit. Very long text is split into segments automatically for synthesis.

Why does the voice sound unnatural?

Try these improvements: choose a voice that matches the text language, adjust speed and pitch appropriately, and make sure punctuation is used correctly, since it affects pauses and intonation.

What is the difference between the browser voice and Edge TTS?

Edge TTS is Microsoft's cloud-based speech synthesis service, offering more natural audio and richer voice choices, but it requires the local backend to be running. The browser's built-in speech engine (Web Speech API) does not need the backend, but quality and available voices depend on your operating system and browser.
